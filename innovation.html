The Thinking Behind
the Marketplace

Target users, pain points, key features, risks, and the experiment we're running to find out if we're solving the right problem.

Innovators Hypothesis

Five dimensions, in order. Each one distinct.

1 Target User

Digital Product & IT Leads at brand level

Teams at Scania, MAN, or VW T&B who are actively building and deploying AI agents — and need to move fast without waiting for shared group infrastructure to exist.

2 Pain Point

Agents are siloed with no way to discover or share across brands

No standardized communication layer, no shared governance, no visibility into what exists elsewhere. Cross-brand optimization is blocked and teams keep rebuilding the same capabilities independently.

3 Key Feature

A cross-brand registry with a lightweight governance layer

Make agents across all TRATON brands discoverable and interoperable — with a request and approval flow that enforces policy without requiring teams to consolidate infrastructure or give up brand autonomy.

4 Biggest Risk This Sprint

We might be solving the wrong problem

The real friction may be political or organizational, not technical. If brands don't want to share agents due to trust or internal incentive structures, a platform solution becomes irrelevant no matter how well it works technically.

5 Experiment to Remove the Risk

TRATON stakeholder interviews

We ask brand leads one focused question designed to separate the technical problem from the organizational one. If discoverability alone unlocks value, people will name concrete actions they'd take. If deeper blockers dominate, they won't.

"If you knew every AI agent that existed across the TRATON group right now — what would you actually do differently?"
