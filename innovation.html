The Thinking Behind

Our hypothesis, what we've validated so far, and what we're building toward next.

Innovators Hypothesis

Five dimensions, in order. Each one distinct.

1 Target User

TRATON Group IT and governance teams

TRATON Group IT, digitalisation strategy, and access-control teams responsible for governing agentic AI across Scania and sister brands.

2 Pain Point

Agents proliferate faster than governance can follow

Shadow agents operate outside the central platform and human-credentialed agent activity is invisible. There is no consistent registry or policy framework for discovering, approving, and governing agent usage across teams and brands.

3 Key Feature

A governance and observability layer for the agent ecosystem

A central registry with approval workflows, policy tagging, cross-brand visibility, and differentiated treatment for agents vs humans — making every agent discoverable, classifiable, and governable without requiring teams to give up autonomy.

4 Biggest Risk This Sprint

Governance tooling is seen as overhead rather than enablement

Teams actively building agents may resist a registry and approval layer if it adds friction without obvious near-term value — especially if Scania's internal platform already covers parts of this ground.

5 Experiment to Remove the Risk

Demonstrate the governance layer to two internal stakeholders

Run targeted conversations with the security/access control team and one business-unit PO, showing the live registry and approval flow. Test whether having a searchable, policy-tagged agent registry with approval states would change how they govern agent deployments today.

"If you could see every agent running across your org right now — with its approval status, policy tags, and owner — what would you do differently with that information?"

Progress So Far

What we've done and what we learned from it.

✓
Step 1 — Completed
Discovery session — tested our understanding of the problem space

Ran an initial session to pressure-test our assumptions — specifically whether the core need was technical or organisational. Confirmed that discoverability and cross-brand coordination are genuine blockers. From this we developed our Point of View statement:

Point of View Statement

Teams working digitally across TRATON need a way to easily find, trust, and reuse existing agents across brands because access restrictions and fragmented systems make reuse harder than rebuilding, leading to duplicated work and inefficiency.

✓
Step 2 — Completed
Built initial prototype

Developed this interactive prototype — an agent registry across all TRATON brands with a live governance demo — to make the concept tangible and discussable with real stakeholders rather than abstract.

✓
Step 3 — Completed
Validated prototype with TRATON — directionally confirmed, new dimensions identified

Tested the prototype with our TRATON point of contact. Feedback was clear: the direction is absolutely right for what they need. Beyond validation, the session surfaced additional dimensions that are key to moving forward and have now been added to our roadmap:

🗺️
Governance & security architecture mapping
A conceptual mapping of how governance, access control, authentication, and security layers fit together in the platform — consulting-style, diagram-driven.
✨
Agent creation in-platform (nice-to-have)
The idea of building and deploying agents directly inside the marketplace resonated strongly. Scope TBD — will be evaluated once core validation is complete.
✓
Step 4 — Completed
Stakeholder testing — economy framing didn't land, refocused on governance

Conducted one interview with a Scania agent expert and the main project POC, and secured a 30-minute diagnostic call with a Scania AI strategist. The token-based reimbursement model was tested and seen as premature — stakeholders redirected back to the governance and observability gap as the real problem. Economy layer deprioritised; prototype refocused on registry and governance.

💡
Scania is already building the skills and agent platform internally
Competing on discoverability alone is no longer defensible. The governance layer wrapping those agents — including shadow ones — is the real gap.
🔄
Economic settlement layer set aside for now
Token-based reimbursement is preserved in version control for potential reintroduction if a future sprint validates the underlying problem.
🎯
Refocused: registry + governance + policy enforcement
Making every agent discoverable, classifiable, and governable is the current core value proposition.
